INDIAN NATIONAL & STATE SCHOLARSHIP INFORMATION HANDBOOK
---------------------------------------------------------

1. NATIONAL SCHOLARSHIP PORTAL (NSP)
The NSP hosts multiple Central Government schemes including:
- Post Matric Scholarship (SC/ST/OBC/Minority)
- Merit-cum-Means Scholarship for Professional Courses
- Pragati Scholarship for Girl Students (AICTE)
- Saksham Scholarship for Students with Disabilities

Eligibility:
- Annual family income thresholds vary (₹1 lakh – ₹8 lakh)
- Must have valid Aadhaar, bank account, income certificate

2. STATE GOVERNMENT SCHEMES
Each state offers additional schemes, such as:
- Fee reimbursement for EWS students
- Scholarships for rural/tribal students
- Caste-category scholarships
- Transport allowance for students living in remote areas

Students should refer to their respective state's education department website annually.

3. CATEGORY-BASED SCHOLARSHIPS
- *SC/ST:* Post-Matric support, book allowances, hostel subsidies
- *OBC/EWS:* Tuition waivers, merit-based scholarships
- *Minority Students:* Maulana Azad Scholarship, Merit-cum-Means support

4. GIRL-SPECIFIC SCHOLARSHIPS
- AICTE Pragati Yojana (₹50,000 per year)
- State-level girl child scholarships
- Kanyashree, Ladli schemes (state-specific)

5. PRIVATE/NGO SCHOLARSHIPS
Many organizations provide scholarships:
- Tata Trusts
- Reliance Foundation
- Sahu Trust
- Buddy4Study linked programs

Typically require:
- Merit + income criteria
- Simple essays or interviews

6. APPLICATION RECOMMENDATIONS
- Keep documents (income certificate, caste certificate) updated yearly
- Apply to at least 2–3 schemes to increase success chance
- Track deadlines (June–December peak period)
