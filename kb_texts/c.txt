UNIVERSITY HOSTEL, RESIDENCE, AND COMMUTE SUPPORT POLICY
---------------------------------------------------------

1. HOSTEL ALLOCATION PRIORITY
Due to limited hostel capacity, priority is given to:
- Female students traveling from distant or unsafe areas
- Students with commute time exceeding 90 minutes one-way
- Rural students with unreliable or infrequent transportation
- Students with documented health or mobility difficulties

2. SPECIAL HOSTEL ALLOTMENT REQUESTS
Students facing academic difficulties due to long travel may apply for special allotment mid-semester.
Required documents may include:
- Residence proof
- Travel time declaration
- Mentor recommendation

3. COMMUTE SUPPORT
For students unable to secure hostel accommodation:
- University maintains a list of vetted PGs and shared accommodations
- Guidance on safe localities, rent expectations, and essential amenities
- Support for forming shared accommodation groups among new entrants

4. HOSTEL CODE OF CONDUCT
- Quiet hours from 10 PM to 6 AM
- Mandatory entry/exit logs for safety
- Ragging, bullying, and intimidation strictly prohibited
- Wardens available for grievance handling
