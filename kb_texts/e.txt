MENTOR PLAYBOOK FOR HANDLING STUDENT FINANCIAL & FAMILY PRESSURE CASES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

1. INITIAL CONVERSATION PROTOCOL
- Let the student speak freely for the first 5–7 minutes
- Ask clarifying questions gently
- Avoid judgment or assumption
- Take notes discreetly

2. FINANCIAL PRESSURE WORKFLOW
A. Determine whether the issue is:
   - Temporary (e.g., salary delay)
   - Long-term (chronic low income)

B. Offer support paths:
   - Fee installment
   - Partial fee waiver recommendation
   - Scholarship application support
   - Connection to senior student volunteers for part-time guidance

3. FAMILY PRESSURE AND EARLY MARRIAGE CASES
- Avoid direct confrontation with parents
- Help the student prepare a structured conversation with family
- Explore involvement of supportive elders
- Encourage emphasizing long-term career and stability benefits

4. WHEN TO ESCALATE
- If threats, coercion, or forced withdrawal are involved
- If the student expresses fear for safety
- If academic participation drops sharply

5. DOCUMENTATION
Mentors should log:
- Meeting summaries
- Student concerns
- Action steps taken
- Next review date
