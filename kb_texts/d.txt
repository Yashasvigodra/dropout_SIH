UNIVERSITY COUNSELLING & WELLBEING SUPPORT HANDBOOK
----------------------------------------------------

1. SCOPE OF COUNSELLING
Counselling services are available for:
- Academic stress
- Anxiety and overwhelm
- Homesickness
- Family conflict affecting academics
- Low confidence and self-esteem
- Focus and motivation issues

2. HOW TO ACCESS COUNSELLING
- Walk-in hours: 10 AM – 5 PM (Monday to Friday)
- Appointment booking through the student portal
- Mentor referral in moderate/high-risk cases

3. MENTOR GUIDELINES FOR IDENTIFYING DISTRESS
Signs include:
- Frequent absenteeism
- Social withdrawal
- Visible emotional exhaustion
- Panic during exams
- Mentions of hopelessness or self-doubt

Mentors should:
- Listen without judgment
- Avoid minimizing (“everyone feels stressed”)
- Encourage small achievable goals
- Recommend counselling early

4. RED FLAG ESCALATION
If a student mentions:
- Self-harm
- Feeling unsafe in hostel/class
- Severe panic attacks
- Thoughts of running away/dropping out suddenly

Then mentor must:
- Ensure the student is not left alone
- Contact counselling cell immediately
- Inform designated authority per protocol

5. CONFIDENTIALITY AND ETHICS
Counsellors maintain confidentiality except in emergencies involving safety risks.
